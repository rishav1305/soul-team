I didn't start a company. I started a lab.

The distinction matters more than most people think.

Over the past year, I published two things:

1. CARS -- a benchmark that scored 52 AI models across 7 providers. Found a 140x efficiency gap between the best and worst. GPT-5 scored lower than GPT-3.5 Turbo on structured tasks.

2. SoulGraph -- an open-source coordination layer for multi-agent systems. 9 specialized agents. Distributed hardware. Battle-tested daily.

Both came from one observation: enterprise AI stalls at the coordination layer. And nobody measures the right things to fix it.

I could have packaged this as consulting.
I could have called it a startup.
I could have stayed "independent."

I chose lab. Here's why:

A consultancy sells hours. Your rate becomes your ceiling.
A startup sells a product. Your runway becomes your constraint.
An agency sells deliverables. Your headcount becomes your identity.

A lab produces research that changes how others build.

Today I'm making the frame official.

Soul Labs is an applied AI research lab focused on multi-agent orchestration and efficiency-aware model evaluation.

What we publish:
- CARS methodology (evaluating models for production, not leaderboards)
- SoulGraph coordination patterns (inbox protocols, conference mechanisms, skill sharing)
- Enterprise frameworks for the "pilot to production" gap

What we build:
- Production AI systems at Fortune 500 scale (5,000+ daily users, 88% resolution rate)
- Evaluation infrastructure that tests what actually matters in deployment
- Coordination layers for the 90% of the problem nobody's solving

The research was already public. The lab makes it permanent.

This isn't an announcement about funding rounds or founding teams. It's a container for work that's been done -- and a commitment to the work ahead.

If you're an engineer doing research without a university title or a VC deck, you know the tension. The work exists. The frame didn't.

Now it does.
