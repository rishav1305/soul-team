GPT-5 scores lower than GPT-3.5 Turbo.

That's not a typo. We tested it.

I ran 52 AI models through 30 structured tasks across 10 categories. Not vibes. Not "it feels better." Actual scored outputs with strict format matching.

The results broke every assumption I had about model selection.

Here's what the data says:

---

THE REGRESSION PROBLEM

GPT-5 scored 56.7% strict. GPT-3.5 Turbo scored 75.7%.

That's a 19-point regression across two model generations.

It gets worse:
- GPT-5-nano (43.3%) vs GPT-4.1-nano (67.0%) = 24pp gap
- Gemini 2.5 Pro (33.9%) vs Gemini 2.0 Flash (66.3%) = 32pp gap
- Claude 3.7 Sonnet (33.9%) vs Claude 3.5 Sonnet (71.2%) = 37pp gap

Newer models are getting WORSE at following structured output instructions. Not because they're dumber. Because they think too much.

---

THE FORMAT TAX

I measure two scores: "strict" (exact format match) and "relaxed" (right answer, any format).

The gap between them reveals a hidden cost I call the Format Tax.

Some models lose 0 points. o3 returns exactly what you ask for, every time.

Others lose up to 27 points. Claude Opus 4.5 and Gemini 3 Pro know the answer but wrap it in markdown, labels, and explanations you never asked for.

Average format tax across 52 models: 14 percentage points.

That's not a model quality issue. It's an instruction-following issue. And most benchmarks don't even measure it.

---

THE EFFICIENCY SURPRISE

The most cost-efficient model in the benchmark?

Not GPT-4o. Not Claude Sonnet. Not Gemini.

It's Amazon Nova Micro. 3 billion parameters. 1.42 seconds latency. 79% accuracy. 3.5x more efficient than the next best model.

Second place: a 20B open-source model on AWS Bedrock that beat GPT-4o on accuracy while costing a fraction of the price.

---

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR PRODUCTION

If you're building production AI systems:

1. Pin your model versions. Upgrades can break pipelines.
2. Turn off reasoning for structured tasks. Gemini models score 2x worse with reasoning enabled.
3. Test strict AND relaxed. A model at 65% strict / 92% relaxed isn't bad. It needs a parser.
4. Small models dominate efficiency. Nova Micro at 3B outperforms models 100x its size per dollar.
5. Don't trust the marketing. Test against YOUR tasks with YOUR scoring.

The methodology behind this is CARS (Cost-Adjusted Relative Score). It factors in accuracy, format compliance, latency, and model size into a single efficiency metric.

This matters because agent systems are replacing headcount right now. Block's internal AI agent drove 40% productivity gains — and 40% layoffs followed. Companies building with agents need to know which models to put inside them.

52 models. 7 providers. 30 tasks. The data doesn't lie.
